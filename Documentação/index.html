Central Pattern Control for robots articulated Documentation

Vertebrates and invertebrates animals have some neural circuits that produce rhythmic patterns of neural activities autonomously. These neural circuits are called Central Pattern Generators. In vertebrates, the spinal cord plays the role of CPGs, its existence has already been demonstrated in several of these animals, such as fish, amphibians, cats and humans.

One of the first studies with CPGs were made by Ijspeert. His studies report the application of evolutionary algorithms to adjust the parameters of a neural model of locomotion control of a lamprey in a simulation environment. This neural model was developed from the biological model of lamprey proposed by Ekeberg. Ijspeertetal (1999) also presented an approach using a genetic algorithm to evolve the architecture of a connectionist model able to determine the muscle activity during the execution of the swim of the lamprey.
